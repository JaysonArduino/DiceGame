Computer Programming & Interfacing

Favourite piece of technology

My favourite piece of technology is my laptop, an Apple Macbook Air (13-inch). The Macbook Air is a very thin and lightweight notebook, only weighing 1.35 kg; it practically defines portability and convenience. Despite its compact form factor, its performance is comparable to that of desktop pc towers.

I bring my laptop to school every day, and for the better or worse, it's become an essential tool of mine. I use it for school work, hobbies, talking with friends, catching news, learning , and more!

A couple things that I like about it specifically are its screen with 128 dpi/ppi, macOS, SSD storage, long battery life, and of course its distinguished aesthetics.

Laptops hugely benefit society. The ability to carry a personal computer would be especially useful for students, people in the business industry, etc. The macbook air specifically is used by many professionals, and is well known for its use in productivity. Video editing, for example, is one of its strong points.






New Technology

Intel's Vaunt smart glasses

Intel is bringing out the next generation of smart glasses, and they look just like any other pair of normal glasses. The smart glasses will connect to your smartphone via bluetooth, and information such as notifications, directions, and restaurant ratings can be projected. This means, however, that a (class 1) laser will always be in your eye. So, what sets Vaunt apart from other AR glasses such as Google Glasses? Vaunt doesn't currently have a camera or microphone (which is why Google Glasses are not allowed in some places), draw attention with its out of place looks, and it also weighs 20 grams less. These smart glasses probably won't be released to the general public for a while, but hopefully Intel will make good use of Vaunt's potential.

Things I Found Interesting/Difficult

When I first opened the computer, There were a couple things unfamiliar to me. The components were clearly old, and were not the same as the standard components that I was used to. Some things that surprised me were the use of PATA cables, and the fact that the graphics card didn't need to be connected to the PSU through a PCI connector. Beyond School

Thinking about the places I have been during the last week, it was apparent that people were utilizing technology to carry out their daily jobs. To list a few examples, cashiers at various stores were using barcode scanners, fast food employees were using headsets to communicate with customers and other employees, and taxi drivers were using GPS units for help with navigating. Although these devices may not require much trainingor any training at allthere are definitely cases where technology require much more effort to adapt to. For example, accountants may need up to 2 weeks (total 50 hours) of training to learn how to use accounting software (depending on the software). For such professions, training is crucial, and ignoring it could cause problems later on. If the software were to receive updates or be replaced, say every 10 years, additional training would of course be required.

When I think how the things I am learning in school will help me prepare for future careers, I think of: analytical skills, writing and reading skills, persuasive skills, presentation experience, prioritization and time management skills. While algebra or knowledge of Newtons 3 Laws may not be so crucial in many jobs, the effort and perseverance put into learning them will serve as training. School doesnt teach, however, important life skills. Things like negotiating, networking, and socializing are all interpersonal skills that students like me have to push themselves to gain without any class that directly teaches it. Luckily, there are school clubs that will provide you with that first step I should probably try to get more involved.

The university I would like like to attend is the University of Toronto, and I aim to be admitted into their Computer Science program on St. George Campus. The requirements are an "Ontario Secondary School Diploma: Grade 12 English (ENG4U) and Calculus (MCV4U) plus four additional 4U/M courses." Aside from the required high school courses, I think that beneficial courses would be Computer Science (Grade 11 & 12) and Computer Engineering (Grade 11 & 12) which I plan to finish.



Home Network

A network is basically a system of devices that are all connected to each other, and able to communicate by using the same language (protocol). Take a business network, for example. There would probably a router/modem connecting to various switches/hubs, connecting to computers, servers, a mainframe, databases, and more. All the computers would be connected together, and able to request/receive information from one another.
